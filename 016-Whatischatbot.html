Definition of a Chatbot

A chatbot is a software application designed to simulate conversation with human users, especially over the Internet. It uses artificial intelligence (AI) and natural language processing (NLP) to understand and respond to user queries in a human-like manner. Chatbots are used in various applications, such as customer service, information acquisition, entertainment and used for booking appointments or reservations. Here are three examples of chatbot are:
Customer Service Bots:
These are used by companies on their websites or social media platforms to answer customer queries, assist with bookings, or provide product information. For instance, many airlines have chatbots for handling flight bookings and inquiries. Virtual Assistants:
Examples include Siri by Apple, Alexa by Amazon, and Google Assistant by Google. They use voice recognition and AI to perform tasks like setting reminders, playing music, providing weather updates, and controlling smart home devices. Healthcare Bots:
These are designed to assist patients with medical inquiries, schedule appointments, or even provide preliminary medical advice. Bots like Babylon Health use AI to assess symptoms and offer health-related information.
