Different Types of AI

Based on Functionality: 1. Reactive Machines:
Reactive Machines represent the simplest form of AI, focusing solely on present scenarios and reacting to them. They lack memory-based functionality and cannot use past experiences for future actions. An example is IBM's Deep Blue chess program, which analyzes and responds to the current state of the game without learning. 2. Limited Memory AI: Limited Memory AI can store and utilize past experiences or data for a short time, enhancing decision-making in the present. This type encompasses most current AI applications, like self-driving cars and personalized recommendation systems, which improve their responses or predictions based on accumulated data but lack long-term memory or learning capabilities.
Examples: Self-driving cars, chatbots. 3. Theory of Mind AI:
Theory of Mind AI represents an advanced AI concept aimed at understanding and interpreting human emotions, beliefs, and intentions. This type of AI aspires to interact socially and emotionally like humans, recognizing and responding to human psychological states. However, it remains largely theoretical and under development in the AI research community. 4. Self-aware AI:
Self-aware AI is a theoretical concept where AI systems possess their own consciousness and self-awareness. Such AI would have a deep understanding of its own existence, emotions, and internal states, mirroring human self-awareness. This advanced form of AI, still in the realm of speculation, raises profound ethical and philosophical questions.

Each type represents different stages and potentials in AI development, with varying levels of complexity, autonomy, and capabilities.
